The honest conversation

"I run my business independently.
I don't need external software."

Thanks for reaching out but I'm running my own cleaning business independently and not looking to use external software or platforms, but I appreciate you thinking of me.

A DM received last week  ·  a genuinely fair response

You built your cleaning business yourself. No franchise, no formula handed to you, no safety net. That's genuinely impressive — and worth protecting. So when someone pitches you software, the reaction makes sense. You've probably seen tools that promised to save time and cost you more of it.

Here's the honest thing we'd say back: you already use external software. Your banking app tells you what's come in. WhatsApp is how bookings get confirmed. Your phone calendar manages your week. Maybe a spreadsheet handles invoices, or maybe you do it by hand. Maybe your notes app is where client details live. Every one of those is an external platform — you just don't think of them that way because they're familiar.

The question isn't whether to use external software. It's whether the software you use was built for your business. Your bank wasn't. Your calendar wasn't. Your spreadsheet definitely wasn't.

Running independently means making your own decisions. Cadi doesn't change that. It means when you decide what to charge, which jobs to take, whether this month adds up — you're making that call with real numbers in front of you instead of a rough feeling. Your clients are yours. Your prices are yours. Your data is yours. Cadi just makes sure you can see all of it clearly.

And if you try it and it's not for you? Free plan, no card, no pressure. You've lost nothing except the twenty minutes it took to set up.

The things people actually ask

I'm not very techy. Will I actually be able to use this?
Yes. Cadi was built for cleaning business owners, not software people. If you can use WhatsApp and a bank app, you can use Cadi. There's no setup complexity — you add your first client, create your first job, and it starts making sense immediately. Most people are up and running in under 20 minutes.
How is Cadi different from FreshBooks or QuickBooks?
Those are general accounting tools built for anyone with a business. Cadi was built specifically for cleaning businesses — so it understands your sectors, your job types, your pricing logic. It handles scheduling, route planning, client management and MTD ITSA alongside the accounting. No other tool does all of that. Accountants love it too — everything is already categorised correctly.
Is the free plan actually useful, or is it just a hook?
It's genuinely useful. Free covers up to 50 customers, scheduling, standard invoicing, the pricing calculator, route planning and a basic tax estimate. For a sole trader just starting out, or anyone who wants to try Cadi properly before committing, free is a real plan — not a 14-day trial with a cliff edge at the end.
What if I want to cancel?
Cancel anytime. No notice period, no exit fee, no trying to talk you round on a retention call. If you're on a monthly plan, you keep access until the end of your billing period. Your data is yours — you can export everything before you go. We'd rather earn your loyalty every month than trap you into staying.
