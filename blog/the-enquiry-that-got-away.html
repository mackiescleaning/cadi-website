The enquiry that got away

Every cleaning business owner has lost a job they never even knew about. The enquiry came in at the wrong time, went unanswered for a few hours, and they booked someone else. You didn't lose to a better cleaner. You lost to a faster one.

R
Rhi
6 minute read · Growth & lead conversion

There's a particular kind of revenue loss that doesn't show up anywhere. It doesn't appear on your profit and loss. It doesn't register as a cancellation. There's no notification, no voicemail, no record of it ever existing. It's the enquiry that came in while you were on a job — the person who found you on Google, got excited, typed out a message asking about a regular clean, and then received nothing back for four hours.

By the time you replied, they'd already booked someone else. They didn't tell you. They just didn't respond to your follow-up. And you'll never know how much of your potential revenue disappears this way every single month.

The five-minute window

There's a well-documented pattern in sales research: the probability of converting an inbound lead drops dramatically the longer you wait to respond. The first five minutes after someone sends an enquiry are worth more than the next five hours combined. After an hour, the conversion rate has fallen off a cliff.

This isn't because people are impatient. It's because they're searching for a solution to a real problem right now — and when they find you, they're ready to move. While they're waiting for you to reply, they're still on Google. They're finding someone else. And that someone else — the one who responds in two minutes — gets the job.

"You didn't lose to a better cleaner. You lost to a faster one. Speed of response is often the only differentiator that matters in the first touch."

The frustrating thing is that you're not slow because you don't care. You're slow because you're physically on a job, driving between clients, managing your team, doing everything that running a cleaning business actually requires. You can't be in two places at once. But your response time doesn't need you to be.

What the lost enquiry actually costs

Let's be conservative. Say you lose three enquiries a month to slow response. Each one was a potential regular domestic client — someone who would have booked fortnightly at £90 a visit. That's three clients gone before they ever started.

£7k
Approximate annual revenue lost from just three unresponsive enquiries per month — each a potential fortnightly client at £90 a visit. And that's the conservative estimate.

Three clients at £90 fortnightly is £180 a month per client. That's £540 a month, £6,480 a year — before you factor in any upsells, referrals they might have made, or the compounding effect of a growing recurring client base. The number is almost certainly larger than it feels like it should be.

And again — you'll never see this number anywhere. It's invisible. It doesn't hurt in any specific, measurable way. It just means your business grows more slowly than it should, and you never quite know why.

The problem with "I'll reply when I get a moment"

Most cleaning business owners handle enquiries reactively. A message comes in via Facebook, WhatsApp, the contact form, or a missed call. It sits there until there's a gap in the day — between jobs, during lunch, in the car before the next one. By then, it's been an hour. Maybe two. Maybe more if it came in at 7pm and you're not checking messages in the evening.

The intention is good. The follow-through is reasonable. But the timing is fatal. The person who sent that enquiry is not waiting patiently for your thoughtful reply. They're getting on with their day, and another cleaning business is already in their inbox.

This isn't a personal failing. It's a structural problem. You can't reply instantly to every enquiry when you're physically occupied. The only solution is to make your response speed independent of your availability.

The compounding effect of consistency

There's another dimension to this that's easy to miss. When every enquiry gets an instant, professional response, your reputation changes. Google reviews start mentioning it. "Replied really quickly." "Got back to me straight away." "So easy to get in touch with." These aren't just nice words — they're the signals that move you up the local search rankings and give future prospects the confidence to choose you over a competitor with similar prices.

Reviews are increasingly the main conversion lever for local cleaning businesses. And responsiveness is one of the most consistent things people mention in five-star reviews. Fix the response speed, and the downstream effects ripple outward further than you'd expect.

The enquiry that doesn't get away

The shift is simple in principle: your response speed stops being a function of your availability. Whether the enquiry comes in at 9am on a Monday or 10pm on a Sunday, it gets a real, useful reply within seconds. The conversation has started before you even know the lead exists.

That's not automation for automation's sake. That's making sure the revenue your marketing generates actually reaches your business — instead of quietly disappearing into someone else's booking calendar while you're doing your actual job. The best cleaning businesses don't just get more leads. They convert a higher proportion of the leads they already have.
