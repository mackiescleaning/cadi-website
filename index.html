FAQ

Questions
cleaning businesses
actually ask.

Everything you need to know before getting started — from HMRC to pricing to switching from spreadsheets.

What is Making Tax Digital (MTD) and does Cadi handle it?
Making Tax Digital for Income Tax Self Assessment (MTD ITSA) is HMRC's new system requiring sole traders and landlords earning over £50,000 to submit quarterly digital tax updates from April 2026 (£30,000 from April 2027). Cadi is directly integrated with HMRC's MTD API — your income and expenses are tracked in real time and quarterly submissions are sent with a single click. No accountant needed, no spreadsheets, no surprises.
How much should I charge for a house clean in the UK?
Most independent cleaners in the UK charge between £16–£28 per hour depending on location, experience and specialism. For a 3-bedroom home, a regular clean typically runs 2.5–3 hours (£45–£84). One-off cleans command a 50% premium; deep cleans around 2× and end-of-tenancy cleans 3× the hourly rate. Cadi's pricing calculator factors in your rate, property size and clean type to give you a suggested price that covers your time and costs.
What expenses can I claim as a self-employed cleaner?
As a self-employed cleaner you can claim: cleaning products and equipment, mileage (45p/mile for the first 10,000 miles), uniform and protective clothing, phone costs (business proportion), insurance, software subscriptions (including Cadi), training and professional development, and a proportion of home office costs if you work from home. Cadi tracks all allowable expenses automatically and categorises them correctly for your Self Assessment return.
Do I need to register for VAT as a cleaning business?
You only need to register for VAT once your turnover exceeds £90,000 in a 12-month rolling period (2024/25 threshold). Most independent cleaning businesses sit well below this. Cadi monitors your rolling turnover and will alert you if you're approaching the threshold so you're never caught out.
Is Cadi suitable for sole traders and small cleaning teams?
Yes — Cadi was designed specifically for sole traders and small cleaning businesses with up to 10 staff. Whether you're running 5 clients or 50, the same platform handles your scheduling, invoicing, expenses and HMRC reporting. Solo operators get the full feature set at the same price.
How does Cadi help me find more cleaning clients?
Cadi includes a local leaderboard that benchmarks your business health score against other cleaning businesses in your area. A higher score improves your visibility within the Cadi network. Combined with automated review requests sent after each job, professional invoices and a client-facing booking portal, Cadi helps you look and perform like an established business — which is the best client-acquisition tool there is.
What happens if I miss a quarterly MTD submission deadline?
HMRC operates a points-based penalty system for MTD ITSA. Each missed quarterly deadline earns one penalty point; four points in a 12-month period triggers a £200 fine. Additional daily penalties apply for returns more than 15 days late. Cadi sends you reminders well before each deadline and keeps your submission data ready to go — so missing a deadline is genuinely avoidable.
Can I switch to Cadi if I'm already using another accounting tool?
Yes. You can import your existing client list and transaction history into Cadi. If you're using QuickBooks, FreeAgent or a spreadsheet, our onboarding team will help you migrate cleanly. You don't lose any historical data and your MTD connection can be transferred at any point in the tax year.
What areas of the UK does Cadi cover?
Cadi is built for cleaning businesses across the whole of the United Kingdom — England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland. The platform is calibrated to UK tax rates, HMRC deadlines, UK mileage allowances and UK employment law. The local leaderboard currently covers major towns and cities including London, Manchester, Birmingham, Leeds, Bristol, Edinburgh, Cardiff, and Milton Keynes, with more areas added monthly.
